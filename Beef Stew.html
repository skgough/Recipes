Beef StewHome

4 servings
2 hours 30 minutes
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1/4 tsp freshly ground pepper
1 tsp salt
1 lb beef stewing meat, trimmed and cut into inch cubes
3 tbsp red wine vinegar
1 cup red wine
3 cups beef broth, homemade or low-sodium canned
3 bay leaves
1 tsp herbes de Provence
1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
1 tbsp soy sauce
1 medium onion, diced
3 cloves garlic, minced
5 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 1/4 inch rounds
2 large baking potatoes, peeled and cut into 3/4 inch cubes
  1. Combine 1/4 cup flour, 1 tsp salt and 1/4 tsp pepper in a bowl, add the beef and toss to coat well. Heat oil in a large pot. Add the beef a few pieces at a time; do not overcrowd. Cook, turning the pieces until beef is browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per batch; add more oil as needed between batches.
  2. Remove the beef from the pot and throw in the onion and saute until starting to caramelize, about 15 minutes. Toss in garlic and cook until fragrant. Add the beef, beef broth, 3 tbsp vinegar, 1 cup wine, 3 bay leaves, 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp herbes de Provence and 1/4 tsp cayenne. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer.
  3. Cover and simmer, skimming broth from time to time, until the beef is tender, about 1 1/2 hours.
  4. After 1 1/2 hours, add the carrots, and simmer covered for 10 minutes. Add the potatoes and simmer until vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes more. Add broth or water if the stew is dry. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve.
